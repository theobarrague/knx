Dear Reader,

The set of documents you are currently browsing consitutes the first full release of the specification of the KNX system for intelligent homes and buildings, proposed to you by the Konnex Association. These specifications are the output of a collective effort on the part of our Association and its core member companies, representing over 20  person-years of intensive work. They will accompany your company's strategists and developers throughout the process of planning, designing, developing and testing smart building products.

For any building, no matter whether commerical or residential, individual or collective, small-scale or large, public or private - KNX covers the full width and depth of integrated automation and control applications, including:

     lighting
     shading, shutters and blinds
     household appliances and white goods
     heating, cooling
     ventilation and airconditioning
     access and security
     ...
Now clearly, the situation where you decide to buy a new KNX diswasher and have it installed in your family home, is definitely different from the one where a system integrator equips your company's corporate headquarters with thousands of smart KNX components. In between these extremes, we find the typical professional contractor who installs electrical, heating or cooling equipment. It is obvious that to cover such a range, not only the applications themselves have to be addressed: KNX indeed offers several Configuration Modes, catering for these various needs - and does so in a compatible way, encouraging rich cross-interaction between all subsystems,
irrespective of their application domain or  configuration Mode.

Even though all off this is truly a historical first, KNX in actual fact consolidates a wealth of field experience and dominant market presence, collected over more than 12 years by the Konnex legacy associations(*) and their members. So none of the material you will find here is untested, hypothetical or academic; the volumes in front of you incorporate this established know-how, and extend it. And rest assured that the commitment shown by so many leading companies in the preparation of this specification was only given with hard,
commercial objectives in mind. To put it simply: this stuff is real!

Underscoring this statement even more forcefully, you will find a substantial amount of hardware,
safety and no-nonsense test specifications here.
The latter translate the formal system description - itself crystallised around product-centric requirement Profiles -  into a set of operational
and measurable requirements. Fulfill these through our Konnex Certification, the principles of which are also described in these volumes, and you can adorn your product with the coveted KNX conformity label.
We would like to express our gratitude to the numerous experts of so many companies, who contributed to this specification, in particular via the various Konnex Working Groups and Task Forces.

All of us who were involved in producing, reviewing and editing these documents would like you to join in and profit from this unique offer. Welcome to the world of Konnex.

Brussels, March 2002
