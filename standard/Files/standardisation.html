KNX and Standardisation

Konnex Association has been accepted in 2000 by CENELEC as cooperating organisation in the field of Home and Building Electronic Systems (HBES), and is therefore entitled to propose new inputs for standardisation within this European Standardisation Organisation.

Since then, Konnex Association has submitted several parts of the KNX standard to the CENELEC Bureau Technique (BT) for incorporation into the body of European Standards (EN) and in particular its EN 50 090 series. BT in turn has forwarded these Konnex Association proposals for standardisation within CENELEC Technical Committee (TC) 205 “Home and Building Electronic Systems” (HBES).

After successful approval by the European National Committees, these KNX standard proposals have been ratified by the CENELEC BT and thus became the European Standard for HBES.

The set is included in the EN 50090 series and comprises:

  • EN 50090-3-2:
 “Home and Building Electronic Systems, HBES
Part 3-2: Aspects of application – User Process for HBES Class 1”
  • EN 50090-4-1:
 “Home and Building Electronic Systems, HBES
Part 4-1: Medium independent layers – Application layer for HBES Class 1”
  • EN 50090-4-2:
 “Home and Building Electronic Systems, HBES
Part 4-2: Medium independent layers  - Transport layer, network layer and general parts of data link layer for HBES Class 1”
  • EN 50090-5-2:
  “ Home and Building Electronic Systems, HBES
Part 5-2: Media and media dependent layers – Network based on HBES Class 1, Twisted Pair”
  • EN 50090-7-1:
  “Home and Building Electronic Systems, HBES
Part 7-1: System Management – Management Procedures”

The set of standards is currently proposed also for standardisation in CEN and ISO.

Other parts - to be introduced later - are still processed within CENELEC or under consideration within Konnex Association and will soon join the set of standards.

Konnex Association will continue its work of improving and extending the KNX standard and will put forward mature and useable documents for European Standardisation.
